# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors and community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, political beliefs, race, religion, sexual identity and orientation, or socioeconomic status.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- **Be Respectful**: Treat all community members with respect and courtesy
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and support people of all backgrounds and identities
- **Be Constructive**: Provide helpful feedback and criticism in a thoughtful manner
- **Be Patient**: Remember that people have different levels of experience and knowledge
- **Be Professional**: Keep communications professional and focused on the project
- **Give Credit**: Acknowledge contributions and ideas from others
- **Ask for Help**: Don't hesitate to ask questions or seek guidance
- **Assume Good Intent**: Interpret actions charitably and assume people mean well

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive comments related to gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, mental illness, neuroatypicality, physical appearance, body size, race, age, regional discrimination, religion, or other identities
- Unwelcome comments regarding someone's lifestyle choices and practices
- Deliberate misgendering, using slurs, or other language intended to harm
- Sharing others' private information without explicit permission (doxxing)
- Sexual harassment or content of a sexual nature
- Sustained disruption of community discussions or events
- Threats of violence or violent language directed against others
- Advocating for or encouraging any of the above behaviors

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all community spaces, including:

- GitHub repositories and discussions
- Issue trackers and pull requests
- Communication channels (Discord, forums, email, etc.)
- Official project events, online and in-person
- Other spaces managed by or on behalf of the project

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official project email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as a representative at an online or offline event.

## Enforcement

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to:

- Remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct
- Temporarily or permanently ban any contributor for behaviors that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by contacting the project maintainer at [frostmute@gmail.com](mailto:frostmute@gmail.com). All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

All reports will be treated with confidentiality. The project maintainers are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

When reporting, please include:

- Your contact information (so we can follow up with you)
- A description of the incident
- Names of those involved (if applicable)
- Any witnesses or evidence
- The impact the behavior had on you

## Consequences

Community members who violate this Code of Conduct may face:

- A private warning or conversation
- A public warning
- Temporary suspension from community spaces
- Permanent removal from the community

The severity of the response will be proportionate to the severity of the violation and the individual's history in the community.

## Appeals

If a community member feels they have been unfairly moderated, they may appeal the decision by contacting the project maintainer with details about the decision and why they believe it was unfair.
